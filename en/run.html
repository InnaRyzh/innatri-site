ONLINE RUNNING COACH

The time has come when running has evolved from a trendy hobby into a true lifestyle. Every runner pursues their own goal -- from getting healthier and losing weight to completing a marathon or finishing an Ironman. But for everyone, those minutes alone with yourself hold a certain magic.

It is the time when you listen to yourself and tune into your body, when you are alone with your thoughts, when you can ignore phone calls and disconnect from reality without noticing time passing. Or, on the contrary, you can listen to an interesting podcast or audiobook, brainstorm an idea, or find a solution to a problem during your run.

Feeling good -- run. Feeling down -- run even more. You will come back from your run a different person.

Having been involved in various sports all my life, I have always run, and to this day a morning run remains my source of inspiration. It is a cure for all ailments -- your dietitian, your cosmetologist, and your therapist all in one.

But, as you might expect, not everything comes easy from the first step or the first kilometer. Especially if you are over 30 and the last time you ran was in a PE class at school.

You can certainly run on your own after reading a couple of books, diving into the subject, and doing your research. I know people who started on their own and continue running by instinct, earning nothing but my respect. However, most of us are naturally inclined toward inertia -- not everyone has the time to dig deep into the science of proper, healthy running. And we all know perfectly well that with a teacher or coach, you can achieve results significantly faster and more safely.

I offer you my services as a coach and mentor. You can read more about why you need a coach here.

How Online Running Coaching Works

Online coaching is not a generic plan from the internet. It is a fully personalized experience built on analysis of your real data. Each week I create your training plan in TrainingPeaks, taking into account your current fitness, goals, and life schedule.

After every workout, data from your watch (heart rate, pace, cadence, power, elevation) syncs automatically to TrainingPeaks. I analyze each session, provide feedback, and adjust the plan based on real metrics — not guesswork.

My athletes live across multiple time zones — from Moscow to Dubai and New York. The online format lets me work with each person as closely as if we were training on the same track.

Who Online Running Coaching Is For

  • Beginner runners — want to start running properly and safely, without injuries or overtraining
  • First race preparation — planning your first 5K, 10K, half marathon, or marathon
  • Experienced runners — want to reach the next level and set a new personal record
  • Coming back after a break — returning from injury, having a baby, or simply lost motivation
  • Triathletes — need the running component as part of triathlon preparation

What Is Included in Online Coaching

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need running experience to start coaching?
No, experience is not required. I work with runners of all levels — from complete beginners to experienced marathoners. Each athlete receives an individual plan tailored to their current fitness and goals.
How does online running coaching work?
Each week you receive an individual training plan in TrainingPeaks. After each workout, data from your watch syncs automatically. I analyze heart rate, pace, cadence, and other metrics. We discuss results and adjust the plan together.
What watch and apps do I need?
A GPS watch with a heart rate monitor (Garmin, Polar, COROS, or Apple Watch) and the free TrainingPeaks app. Your watch syncs data automatically after each workout.
Can I train for a marathon with an online coach?
Yes, online marathon preparation is one of the most popular requests. I create a 16-24 week plan including endurance building, speed work, long runs, and a race-day nutrition strategy.
How much does online running coaching cost?
Personal online coaching starts at $150 USD per month. See the pricing page for plan details and comparisons.
How soon will I see results?
Initial improvements within 3-4 weeks of consistent training: heart rate stabilizes, technique improves, and confidence grows. Significant pace and endurance progress after 2-3 months of systematic work.
